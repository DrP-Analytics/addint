Machine-collected items from public feeds (Federal Register, openFDA, and growing). Not reviewed; most are routine. The curated cards above are what matter clinically.

What ADDINT is: a physician-curated translation layer over public drug-supply surveillance (CFSRE NPS Discovery, EUDA, CDC, SAMHSA, NDEWS, and others). Every card links its primary source. Signals from online discussion alone are never promoted to alerts without physical (laboratory) corroboration.

What it is not: a complete epidemiological picture (drug-checking samples are self-selected), a substitute for your health department, poison center, or clinical judgment — or a source of information about obtaining any substance.
